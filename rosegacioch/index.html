A Profile of Rose Gacioch

Rose Gacioch was a baseball player in the All American Girls Professional Baseball League from 1944-54. She was born in Wheeling, West Virginia on August 31st, 1915, and died in the Detroit metro area in 2004. Gacioch played for the Rockford Peaches, the team featured in “A League Of Their Own”, for 9 straight seasons. In her time in the AAGPBL, she achieved an ERA below 2.0 in 3 seasons and held the League record in assists in the outfield, while still being known as a power-hitter.

Gacioch was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1988 for her career in the AAGPBL. The Wheeling, West Virginia native remarked at the time that “Now I got something on Pete Rose. I got there before he did.” This attitude of being able to do anything men could do in baseball and in real life persisted throughout her time both on and off the field. For example, Gacioch worked at a factory that made parts for Chrysler automobiles in the off-season.

Players like Rose Gacioch were the trailblazers for modern American women’s athletics and typically were also prime examples of the evolution of gender roles in their time. Given that we’re supposed to be examining American history through baseball, I’m positive that Gacioch would be an interesting player to study for the Baseball Profile as she would be a great way to look at how America changed during and post-World War II with respect to the rise of second wave feminism.
